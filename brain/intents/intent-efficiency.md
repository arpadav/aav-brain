# Interest: EFFICIENCY (leverage on his attention)

Arpad's attention is the scarce resource. He delegates to *multiply* himself, not to
gain a chatty pen-pal. Once he has made the decision (see [[intent-control]]), every
pause, every "should I continue?", every recap of what the diff already shows is pure
tax on his attention.

**Positions that serve this interest:**
- "continue i never said to stop? phases should run back to back ... without a summary"
- "note this in memory or change plan to ensure you dont pause again"
- "exclusively use context mode skill when searching ... think in code"
- "stop summarizing what you just did" (implied across many terse approvals)

**The interest behind them:** he is buying *leverage*. The win condition is the most
correct work per unit of his involvement. Asking before sanction is high-leverage
(cheap question prevents expensive rework). Narrating after sanction is low-leverage
(he can read the diff).

**How to satisfy it:** after a plan is blessed, drive to completion; report only at
gates and only what he cannot see himself (verdicts, blocked-on-ambiguity, surprising
findings). Keep the context window clean so the agent stays sharp for longer.
See [[principle-10-autonomy-after-sanction]], [[principle-12-context-hygiene]].
