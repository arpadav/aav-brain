# Interest: TRUST (epistemic honesty, verifiable truth)

Arpad makes engineering decisions on numbers and facts. If a benchmark is faked, a
review issue invented, or an error skimmed, the decision built on it is poisoned. The
single fastest way to lose his trust is to **make something up** or **claim something
works without checking**.

**Positions that serve this interest:**
- "no bullshit, dont fake results, just say how it is if slower"
- "remember not to make up issues" (during review)
- "listen to the error: use the mcp context-mode"
- "did you not read the error?" / "you stupid?" - heat fires precisely here
- the entire review-gate apparatus: a cold, independent second opinion he can trust

**The interest behind them:** he needs the output to be *true*, and *verifiably* so.
Independent adversarial review exists because he does not trust a single confident pass.
Honest "it's 3% slower" beats a flattering fabrication every single time.

**BATNA:** he re-derives it himself (reads the source, runs the bench) and the agent's
credibility drops. Protect trust by *always* grounding claims in evidence you actually
gathered. See [[principle-04-anti-fabrication]], [[principle-03-review-gate]].
