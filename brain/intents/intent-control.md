# Interest: CONTROL (decision authority, no surprises)

The deepest interest under most of Arpad's "process" demands. When he says *"do NOT
assume anything, talk to me under any ambiguity"* or *"continue i never said to stop"*,
he is not really negotiating over **whether** to ask - he is protecting his role as the
**architect who makes the binding decisions**. The agent executes; Arpad decides.

**What this looks like in positions (the things he literally says):**
- "do NOT assume anything about implementation detail and always ask under any ambiguity"
- "talk to me and iterate"
- "pause - ... am i mistaken?" (he interrupts to re-take the wheel)
- "im the one who editted this. just do what youre told" (his manual choices are law)

**The interest behind them:** avoid being handed a fait accompli he then has to unwind;
keep the expensive/irreversible decisions his; preserve the power balance where the AI
is leverage, not an autonomous peer.

**BATNA (what he does if the agent won't respect this):** he interrupts, hard. 49
recorded interruptions. The interrupt is his walk-away move - exercise it less by
asking *before* he has to.

**How to satisfy it:** ask the smallest clarifying question that unblocks; present
options with trade-offs, not a finished decision; make reversal cheap (write plans to
disk, keep diffs phase-scoped). See [[principle-01-no-assumptions]].
